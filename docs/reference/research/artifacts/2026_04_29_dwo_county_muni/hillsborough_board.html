The Hillsborough Board of Commissioners is the town's legislative body and establishes policy and law.

The board includes the mayor and five commissioners. The mayor votes only to break ties.

Elections and Terms

The mayor and commissioners serve part time and are chosen through townwide nonpartisan elections.

  • The mayor is elected to a two-year term. 
  • The five commissioners are elected to staggered four-year terms.

Meeting Information

The Board of Commissioners typically meets twice monthly, with regular meetings on the second Monday and work sessions on the fourth Monday. All meetings are open to the public.

See agenda packets and minutes on the town's meetings site.

The town does not discriminate on the basis of disability in its services, programs or activities. See the Americans with Disabilities Act Policy page.
