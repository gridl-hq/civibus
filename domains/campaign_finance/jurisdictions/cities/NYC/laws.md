# New York City campaign-finance law notes

This file supports the `laws` section in `config.yaml`.

## Authoritative sources
- NYC Campaign Finance Board: `https://www.nyccfb.info/`
- CFB Rules and Law: `https://www.nyccfb.info/law/rules/`
- NYC Charter Chapter 46 (Campaign Finance): `https://www.nyccfb.info/law/`

## Contribution limits
- Citywide offices (Mayor, Public Advocate, Comptroller): $2,000 per contributor per election.
- Borough President: $1,500 per contributor per election.
- City Council: $1,000 per contributor per election (varies by program participation).
- Corporate contributions to city candidates are prohibited.
- Limits may be adjusted periodically by the CFB.

## Reporting periods
- Semi-annual, pre-election, post-election, 24-hour, and 27-day post-election filings.
- Electronic filing is mandatory for NYC campaign finance disclosure.

## Public financing
- NYC administers a public matching funds program through the Campaign Finance Board.
- Small-dollar contributions (up to $250) from NYC residents are matched at 8:1 for participating candidates.
- The matching funds program is mandatory for candidates who opt in; non-participants still file with CFB.
